The plan.

Stop one stays in Narita — the temple town is genuinely good. Stop two starts from Shinagawa station, five minutes downhill from your bed.

Stop one · Sunday night

Check in, eat, wind down.

You're at the Garden Hotel by about 18:30, only an hour ahead of Singapore. The restaurant does a crew menu at ¥1,300 nett until 22:00, or taxi ten minutes into town for an izakaya. Get the crew lounge password from the front desk — it's on the 2nd floor, open 24 hours. Note: no food delivery to this hotel.

Stop one · Monday

Naritasan Shinshō-ji.

A thousand-year-old Shingon temple ten minutes from the hotel by taxi — great hall, three-storied pagoda, and a big quiet garden behind it. It's the best temple most tourists never see, because they're all in a departure lounge next door.

Unagi on the old road.Queue early

Omotesando — the sloping street between Keisei Narita station and the temple — is Narita's eel row. Kawatoyo has been splitting and grilling unagi in its shopfront window since 1910. Get your name down close to the 10:00 opening or expect a wait; a dozen other eel houses line the same street if the queue is silly.

Stop two · Friday

Thursday night: land, ride in, basement dinner.

This time the bus takes you into the city — about 90 minutes to the Grand Prince Hotel New Takanawa in Shinagawa, in by roughly 19:45. There's a 7-Eleven in the hotel basement (07:00–01:00) and no room service, so konbini dinner is the honest move. Early night — Friday is the day.

Hotel by ~19:457-Eleven on B1F

Sensō-ji, before the crowds win.

From Shinagawa the Keikyū through-train runs direct onto the Asakusa line — about 30 minutes door to door. Kaminarimon gate, the snack stalls of Nakamise-dōri, the incense cauldron, the great red hall. Walk two minutes to the Sumida river afterwards for the straight-shot view of Tokyo Skytree.

Ueno and Ameyoko.

Four stops on the Ginza line from Asakusa. Ameyoko market runs under the rail tracks — street food, snacks, cosmetics, cheap everything. If you want air-con and art, the national museums are across Ueno Park.

Shibuya at dusk.

Now that you live on the Yamanote line, Shibuya is easy — about 25 minutes from Ueno, and only 13 minutes home to Shinagawa afterwards. The scramble crossing as the lights come on, then Shibuya Sky if you want the open-air roof 229 metres up: book a dusk slot online earlier in the day, from about ¥2,500.

Stop two · Saturday

The Takanawa slow morning.

Sleep in. If you want one last quiet thing, Sengaku-ji — the temple of the 47 rōnin — is an eight-minute walk from the hotel. Then Shinagawa station for lunch and the omiyage run (the ecute halls cover KitKats to Tokyo Banana), pack properly, and be in the lobby before the 16:15 pickup. Call is 15:15. If things change

Plan B, C, and rain.

Early July is the tail of tsuyu — the plan assumes showers are possible any day. Drop a level guilt-free.

If it's pouring

Rainy-season downpours are heavy but usually short.

  • Naritasan in drizzle is actually atmospheric — go anyway with an umbrella.
  • In the city, swap outdoors for Ueno's museums or the covered malls at Shinagawa station.
  • Skip Shibuya Sky entirely — no view, no point.

If you're tired (Plan B)

The LAX legs bite. Shrink each stop's radius.

  • Stop one: skip the temple, long unagi lunch, back to bed.
  • Stop two: keep Friday inside the Yamanote — pick one of Asakusa or Shibuya, not both.
  • Sengaku-ji and the station food halls make a full, gentle Saturday on their own.

If you're completely destroyed (Plan C)

Two Pacific crossings in a week. Sometimes the body wins.

  • Don't leave the hotel. At the Prince there's a 7-Eleven downstairs; at the Garden, the crew lounge.
  • Tokyo has waited four hundred years. It will wait for the next NRT.

Before you land

Logistics.

Phone and apps

No firewall here — everything works. Roaming or a cheap eSIM covers both stops. Google Maps handles trains perfectly; Apple Maps works for everything on this page.

Paying for things

Add a Suica to Apple Wallet before you land — it pays for trains, buses, konbini, and vending machines. Cards work in most places now, but keep ¥5,000–10,000 cash for small restaurants and temple stalls.

Early-July weather

Tail end of the rainy season: 27–33°C, sticky, with sudden showers possible. Pack a compact umbrella and breathable clothes. See the rain plan above if a day turns.

Getting around

Stop one: the Garden Hotel's free shuttle runs to AEON Mall and the airport (timetable at the desk); taxis handle the short hop into town. Stop two: Shinagawa station is five minutes' walk — Yamanote line for the loop, Keikyū/Asakusa-line through-trains straight to Sensō-ji.

Crew notes

Two different hotels, two different pickups — 16:45 Monday, 16:15 Saturday. Garden Narita: 24h crew lounge on 2F (password at the desk), crew menu ¥1,300, breakfast ¥1,810, no food delivery. Grand Prince: 7-Eleven on B1F, external delivery OK (collect at front desk), free gym access, 10% off F&B. Carry your Shore Pass with your passport at all times — losing it is a ¥100,000 problem.
