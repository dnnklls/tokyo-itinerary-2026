5–6 & 9–11 July

The plan.

Stop one stays in Narita — the temple town is genuinely good. Stop two goes into Tokyo on the one full day.

Stop one · Sunday night

Check in, eat, wind down.

You're at the hotel by about 18:30 and only an hour ahead of Singapore — no jet lag to fight. Dinner two ways: the hotel restaurant, or a ten-minute taxi to the izakaya and konbini cluster around Keisei Narita station. Ask the front desk for tomorrow's shuttle times while you're checking in.

Hotel by ~18:30Sleep by 23:30
Stop one · Monday

Naritasan Shinshō-ji.

A thousand-year-old Shingon temple ten minutes from the hotel — great hall, three-storied pagoda, and a big quiet garden behind it. Take the hotel shuttle to Keisei Narita station around 09:30, then walk down the old town to the gate. It's the best temple most tourists never see, because they're all in a departure lounge next door.

Unagi on the old road.Queue early

Omotesando — the sloping street between the station and the temple — is Narita's eel row. Kawatoyo has been splitting and grilling unagi in its shopfront window since 1910. Get your name down close to the 10:00 opening or expect a wait; a dozen other eel houses line the same street if the queue is silly.

Ride the Access Express in.

Sensō-ji, before the crowds win.

Step off the train and you're already in Asakusa. Kaminarimon gate, the snack stalls of Nakamise-dōri, the incense cauldron, the great red hall. Walk two minutes to the Sumida river afterwards for the straight-shot view of Tokyo Skytree.

Ueno and Ameyoko.

Four stops on the Ginza line. Ameyoko market runs under the rail tracks — street food, snacks, cosmetics, cheap everything. If you want air-con and art, the national museums are across Ueno Park.

The scramble crossing at dusk, and Shibuya Sky if you want the open-air roof 229 metres up — book a slot online earlier in the day (from about ¥2,500). Be honest about the cost: Shibuya is 40 minutes from Ueno and roughly 90 minutes back to Narita. If you go, leave Shibuya by 20:30.

If things change

Plan B, C, and rain.

Early July is the tail of tsuyu — the plan assumes showers are possible any day. Drop a level guilt-free.

If it's pouring

Rainy-season downpours are heavy but usually short.

  • Naritasan in drizzle is actually atmospheric — go anyway with an umbrella.
  • Swap the Tokyo day for covered ground: Ueno's museums, or stay local at AEON Mall Narita.
  • Skip Shibuya Sky entirely — no view, no point.

If you're tired (Plan B)

The LAX legs bite. Keep everything in Narita.

  • Skip Tokyo on Friday. Do the temple, the garden behind it, and a long unagi lunch instead.
  • AEON Mall for a wander and supplies — the hotel shuttle goes there.
  • Early dinner near the station, in bed by ten.

If you're completely destroyed (Plan C)

Two Pacific crossings in a week. Sometimes the body wins.

  • Don't leave the hotel. Konbini dinner, long bath, sleep.
  • The temple has stood for a thousand years. It will still be there on the next NRT.

Before you land

Logistics.

Phone and apps

No firewall here — everything works. Roaming or a cheap eSIM covers both stops. Google Maps handles trains perfectly; Apple Maps works for everything on this page.

Paying for things

Add a Suica to Apple Wallet before you land — it pays for trains, buses, konbini, and vending machines. Cards work in most places now, but keep ¥5,000–10,000 cash for small restaurants and temple stalls.

Early-July weather

Tail end of the rainy season: 27–33°C, sticky, with sudden showers possible. Pack a compact umbrella and breathable clothes. See the rain plan above if a day turns.

Getting around

The hotel runs a free shuttle to Keisei Narita station and AEON Mall — grab the timetable at the front desk on night one. Trains beat taxis into Tokyo by a mile; taxis are fine for the short hops around Narita town.

Crew notes

Two stops at the same hotel — confirm both pickup times at the first check-in. The NRT–LAX–NRT turnarounds are tight (105 min), so rest properly the nights before each duty. Wake-up calls via the front desk.
